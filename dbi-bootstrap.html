Why I Drag My Bootstrap-Free Feet???

July 31, 2020 by Bunker aka That One Stoner...

This blog post will examine why it's taking me so long to complete the Bootstrap component of the Django Bootcamp... that's providing the structure for this page...

“Only put off until tomorrow what you are willing to die having left undone” ― Pablo Picasso

First off… whole the reason I’m taking this Django Bootcamp is to break free from the boundaries of templated WYSIWYG designs… so I wasn’t all that excited when the instructor highlighted that with Bootstrap… you have to give up many freedoms in exchange for consistent/familiar design…

I’ve never been a huge fan of giving up any form of freedom… which might sound like a strange stance for a shut-in… but I get to hold the control… just as long as I don’t go outside…

Don’t get me wrong… I do see the benefits of Bootstrap in many cases… for example… if I were building sites for clients… where my personal preferences are set aside… I’d use something like Bootstrap to crank out as many client pages as I can…

Even then… I’d probably use WordPress if I were to go the template route… At least that was my thought several years ago when I was first introduced to the format…

Back in 2015… I took my first online web dev bootcamp... in the hopes of opening my options for day jobs... after giving up on my film career… Even back then... I couldn’t stand the automated ways that Bootstrap would readjust content...

I would spend hours attempting to create what I felt was the perfect design fit… only to have things like titles breaking in strange spots… instead of shrinking to fit within the same amount of lines… or images that would only appear on the maximum and minimum page size…

I found that... what was supposed to be a solution... to meet my responsive design need… only led to more work on my end… having to retweak each line to look just right no matter the screen size… keeping in mind that I'm extra picky... due to my OCD...

Inevitably… I’d get everything all set… only to discover that Bootstrap had yet another screen dimension that I didn’t plan for… or that all but one div had been accounted for…

I never thought I would ever use Bootstrap again… that is… until I found it in the line up on the Bootcamp syllabus… though not excited… I am interested in getting a reminder of how the framework might help with responsive design… since isit420ornot.com went off the rails as it became about wacky art and no longer a practice in marketable design...

I'm going to commit to building for broader audience this go around… and one way of doing so is to give Bootstrap another chance…

I can't wait to see how this goes...

Irregular Scheduled Programming...

August 5, 2020 by Bunker aka That One Stoner...

This blog post will examine how my disinterest in structure stems back to a hatred of bedtime...

“Learn the rules, break the rules, make up new rules, break the new rules.” ― Marvin Bell

My hatred of structure goes back to when I was a little kid… to this day… I’m not a fan of hour-long television shows… In my youth… the hour-long shows started at 10:00 in the PM… just after the primetime sitcoms…

This change in program length meant that it was time for me to go to bed… I never liked the idea of a set time for slumber… especially back then… when I was still filled with enthusiasm about how much television there was to watch…

I don’t know what I hated more…

  • ...Having to sleep based on someone else's schedule…...
  • ...These boring-ass hour-long dramas... in the first place...
  • ...Or seeing the cyclical patterns that rule our lives…...

Keep in mind… this is before there was an internet… when we had less control over how we experienced time… The moment I received a TV for my room… my parent lost full control of my television viewing habits… and I’ve had sleep issues ever since… so I do understand that there are benefits of structure…

That said… I feel like I’ve lived a more interesting existence by determining my guidelines on the fly… As much as I may rant and rave about my anti-structure ways… I’m by no means saying that no rules should be the new rule… In fact… I don’t mind having a lack of competition when it comes to experimenting with style… it makes it much easier to stand out from the templated crowd…

BootStrap Conclusions...

August 15, 2020 by Bunker aka That One Stoner

In this blog post... I will share whether or not I plan to use Bootstrap again moving forward...

“I am wary of the whole dreary deadening structured mess that we have built into such a glittering top-heavy structure that there is nothing left to see but the glitter, and the brute routines of maintaining it.” ― John D. MacDonald

This Bootstrap project ended up taking way longer than hoped it would… First off… I picked a template that would force me to interact with the framework language in ways that were covered by the course…

I spent a day or two tweaking the page to get it to look just right in the preview screen… with the false belief to Bootstrap would take care of the responsive design properly for every screen…

Even though the framework language sort of did its job… I was far from impressed by the results… here are a few reasons that I felt let down by the outcome…

  1. The blog titles would not readjust in size to avoid hideous looking line breaks…
  2. Preview images for external articles would only show up on the max and min screen size while staying hidden in sizes between…
  3. Every time that I thought that I fixed the page to look good no matter the size… there seemed to be another screen size unaccounted for…
  4. No matter how much I tweaked and tweaked the layout… I never felt in control of the end results…

Again… I can see the benefits of Bootstrap… If I were building an online store… or content aggregated site… where styling isn’t as important… I might consider using the language more… but since I’m attempting to learn to create art through code… I don’t think I’ll be using this framework moving forward…
