CSS Level One

When I took up screenwriting as an obsession... around a decade and a half ago… I never intended to even visit a film set... I was too big a fan of movies to have the magic of the medium killed...the same way the magic of magic was ruined for me in my youth when I decided to learn a few tricks for my living room performances…

Though I never got the stage presences down… I was quick to pick up on concepts that used to leave me wondering… HOW??? Keep in mind… magic was just a brief interest… movies have always been my life… so when I took up writing films my fantasize of what success looked like... had me handing off a completed script to a crew that I trusted so they would have to interpret my nonsense into art...

I always wrote what I wanted to see... and not what I wanted to make... Because of this… feasibility never ranked that high as a concern… I wanted to leave the impossible up to the movie-making magicians to solve… In a way… I’ve always written as a coder… inputting text through a keyboard to see how it gets interpreted for the screen…

This might be why… back when I took my first coding bootcamp… after blogging with the help of WYSIWYG web builders for a few years… I instantly fell in love with HTML and CSS… unlike the programming languages… where the magic takes place under the hood… there is instant evidence that there is a direct correlation between what you’ve typed up and what you see on the screen…

In many ways... this is exactly what I was chasing when I wrote my first screenplay... only I don’t have to pitch to producers… or wrangle a crew to see the end result… and best of all… I don’t have to hear how me convoluted concepts are impossible… there are just many things that I still need to learn before the potential will be limitless…

Below are the sample assignments from the HTML portion of the Bootcamp... once again… I didn’t use any CSS that wasn’t covered in the course… or didn’t come to me while researching how to get a feature to work… the only thing not covered was the JavaScript that I used to make the button for the Front-End Form fully functional… and not just data that shows up at the end of the URL address… upon hitting submit…

Note: If you're reading this before "I learn" how to make a navbar... there is a button at the bottom of the page that sends you to the next assignment... enjoy!!!
