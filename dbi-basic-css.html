CSS Level One



When I first took up screenwriting as an obsession... I never intended to even visit a film set... I was too big a fan of movies to have the magic of the medium killed for me... When I would fantasize about what success looked like... I would hand off a completed script to a crew that I trusted and have them interpret my nonsense into art... I used to write what I wanted to see... not what I wanted to make...

It turned out that I was right... working in the industry... doing lighting for film and television killed my love for anything to do with cinema... hell... it almost killed my obsession with writing in general... as it started to feel like unless you followed the template of The Hero's Journey... there was no point in creating fictional content...

I'm no hero... I have no interest in their tales... espectially tales told in the same three act structure that we've been stuck with ever since Shakespeare claimed there were no more stories to write... or someone else said he already wrote everything... making originality a meaningless effort... I never like the concept of this quote... so I'm a bit shady on the details...

Well... even if every story possible has already been written... that old dead playwriting fuck never wrote a single line of code... where there is an endless potential for creative expression... and most of the technology and training required to accomplish said creative goals are readily available... and affordable... if not free...

I took a bootcamp similar to this one back when I was looking for a replacement for my quarter of a century looking failed dream... I fell in love with HTML and CSS right away... because here were these text based languages where I could type words into a text editor on a split screen and watch colorful boxes form and even move in the browser that ran on the second half on the screen...

In a way... this is exactly what I was chasing when I first started screenwriting so many years ago... writing words to see how they are interpreted into a visual representation of what I was seeing in my head...

That said... below I have added some CSS to the assignment from the HTML portion of the Bootcamp... I tried to keep it close to what's been covered so far... I only added a couple fancy touches from what I had in my toolbox prior to starting this course...

As I said in the main intro... IsIt420OrNot.com was originally supposed to be this exact same website.. only by the time I got about 50% through the first Bootcamp... I was creating pages that were so unrecognizable from the assignments that the idea of using the Bootcamp's projects as a structure for the site seemed pointless...

That's why this time... I'm going to continue to try to stick to what is being taught and not what I already know...


Note: If you're reading this before "I learn" how to make a navbar... there is a button at the bottom of the page that sends you to the next assignment... enjoy!!!
